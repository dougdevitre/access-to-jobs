# Guide: Staff Onboarding

How to train Job Center staff, career advisors, and case managers to use
Access to Jobs in their daily workflow.

---

## Who This Is For

- Career advisors and counselors at American Job Centers
- WIOA case managers (Adult, Dislocated Worker, Youth programs)
- Business services / employer engagement staff
- SNAP E&T (SkillUP) navigators
- DVOP and CODL veteran employment specialists
- Reentry specialists
- AEL instructors who also do job placement
- Supervisors who want to improve team productivity

---

## The 5-Minute Pitch to Staff

"Access to Jobs is an AI assistant that handles the documentation and
template work so you can spend more time with participants.

It can:
- Write case notes from your verbal summary in 30 seconds
- Generate a tailored resume during a participant appointment
- Draft referral letters to partner agencies
- Give you cold call scripts for employer outreach
- Screen participants for program eligibility in real time

It does NOT replace your judgment, make eligibility determinations,
or access any participant records. It's a drafting tool — you review
and finalize everything."

## Day 1: Core Workflows (30 minutes)

### Workflow 1: Intake Triage (Module 10)

**When:** A new participant walks in or calls.

**How:**
```
You: /intake
AI: [Produces structured intake script with priority screening]
```

**Staff action:** Follow the script. The AI identifies which WIOA track,
co-enrollment programs, and urgency level apply. You enter the results
in your case management system.

**Time saved:** ~10 minutes per intake (structured vs. ad hoc)

---

### Workflow 2: Resume Build During Appointment (Module 2)

**When:** Participant needs a resume.

**How:**
```
You: /resume for [Job Title] — here's the job description: [paste JD]

[AI asks for participant details — name, experience, skills, education]
[AI produces ATS-optimized resume in 60 seconds]
```

**Staff action:** Review with participant. Edit as needed. Print or email.

**Time saved:** ~20-30 minutes per resume (vs. building from scratch)

**Tip:** Paste the full job description — the AI mirrors keywords for ATS optimization.

---

### Workflow 3: Case Notes After Appointment (Module 11)

**When:** Immediately after a participant appointment.

**How:**
```
You: /casenote — I met with [First Name] today for resume review.
     We updated her resume for CNA positions, made 3 job referrals
     to SSM Health, and scheduled a follow-up in 2 weeks. She needs
     to complete her CNA certification first.

AI: [Produces formatted, compliant case note ready for MoJobs entry]
```

**Staff action:** Copy into your case management system. Verify accuracy.

**Time saved:** ~10-15 minutes per case note

---

### Workflow 4: Referral Letter (Module 12)

**When:** Referring participant to a partner agency.

**How:**
```
You: /refer — Refer Maria L. to MVR for vocational assessment.
     She has a documented learning disability and needs help
     identifying accommodations for workplace training.

AI: [Produces formal referral letter with agency-appropriate language]
```

**Staff action:** Add agency contact info, print on letterhead, send.

**Time saved:** ~15 minutes per referral letter

## Common Staff Questions

**Q: Will this replace my job?**
A: No. It handles drafting and documentation. You handle relationships,
judgment, case management decisions, and participant coaching. The AI
cannot access participant records, make eligibility determinations, or
replace the human connection that makes workforce development work.

**Q: Is participant data safe?**
A: Claude does not retain data between sessions. Never enter SSNs, full
birthdates, or other sensitive PII. Use first name + last initial only
in case notes and referral letters.

**Q: What if the AI produces something inaccurate?**
A: Always review outputs before using them. The AI never fabricates
experience or credentials, but it may not know about local program
changes or new employers. You are the final quality check.

**Q: Can I use this with participants in the room?**
A: Yes — that's the ideal workflow for resume building (Module 2) and
program screening (Module 0). It's like having a drafting assistant
in the appointment with you.

**Q: How do I handle sensitive populations?**
A: The AI automatically adjusts tone and content for justice-involved,
veteran, youth, disability, and other barrier populations. You don't
need to do anything special — just mention the participant's situation
and it adapts.
